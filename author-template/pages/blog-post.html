Drafting the Final Battle in Solar Ascendant

October 14, 2025 By Aria Vance 5 min read

When I set out to write the climax of Solar Ascendant, I knew it wasn't going to be easy. We are talking about the collision of three massive starfleets, two very angry elemental gods, and Elara caught right in the middle.

The Architecture of Chaos

I actually had to map this out on a giant whiteboard in my office. I used different colored markers for the Ignis Armada, the Voidborn Remnants, and the rebel faction from Cryonia. The hardest part wasn't the magic system—it was the orbital mechanics.

Because Aethelgard's magic is tied to planetary proximity, every time a ship moved in orbit, the elementals aboard that ship would experience a shift in their power output. I had an entire spreadsheet calculating the gravitational pull of the local moons to ensure the combat physics remained consistent.

"Magic without rules is just convenient storytelling. I wanted the final battle to feel like a high-stakes chess match where the board itself is on fire."

The Emotional Core

But spectacle is nothing without emotional weight. Amidst the exploding dreadnoughts and solar flares, the true climax of the book happens in a small, quiet moment between Elara and Kaelen. I won't spoil anything here, but I will say this: it took me five rewrites to get that scene right. I cried during the fourth pass. I hope you do, too.

Solar Ascendant arrives in bookstores in just a few months. Thank you all for joining me on this incredible journey across the stars.
