From Telescopes
To Typewriters

Before the worlds of Aethelgard were born, Aria spent her nights in a dome observatory staring at the silent data of exo-planets. As an astrophysicist, she searched the cosmos for signs of life. Finding none, she decided to create it.

What began as hypothetical equations and atmospheric calculations in the margins of her research logs slowly evolved into living, breathing solar systems filled with ancient magics and complex societies.

"The universe wasn't empty.
It was waiting for a story."

In 2018, while deployed at the Mauna Kea Observatories, Aria penned the first 50,000 words of Starfall Echoes in a span of three weeks. That initial burst of frantic creation formed the bedrock of the Cosmic Chronicles.

Methodology

World-Building Philosophy

Scientific Realism

Every planet's gravity, atmospheric composition, and orbital mechanics in the Chronicles are calculated based on real-world astrophysical models before a single character is placed on their surface.

Hard Magic Systems

Magic isn't a solution; it's a governed physical property. "Aetheric weave" operates strictly under the law of conservation of energy—what is cast must be paid for in thermal dynamics.

Sociological Depth

Aria constructs entire histories, political schisms, and economic dependencies for her factions to ensure conflicts arise from deeply rooted sociological necessities rather than simple morality.

Behind The Pages

The Sanctuary

When she isn't deep diving into quantum mechanics textbooks or attending sci-fi conventions, Aria operates out of a chaotic but cozy home office in the misty Pacific Northwest.

Her writing companions include an ever-growing wall of string-theory bulletin boards, a collection of replica longswords, and her two fiercely unapologetic cats, Nebula and Quasar—who frequently attempt to edit her manuscripts by walking across her keyboard.
